About

I’ve never followed a straight path, but I've known I wanted to be an artist from the time I was young. My understanding of what that means has evolved over time, shaped by experience and introspection.

I’m drawn to the world in all its forms—nature, technology, people, and the cosmos. My work lives at the intersection of creative expression and self-reflection, taking shape through writing, visual art, astrology, and an evolving relationship with film and storytelling.

I value creativity as a vital part of being human. Much of my inspiration comes from everyday experiences, and I often find myself reflecting on the role I play in the world around me. I exist on the edge between acceptance and quiet rebellion against the status quo. My work is an outlet for my inner world.

Teaching has become an important part of my work as well. I’ve found purpose both in public schools and in developing and leading my own classes. That balance provides structure while fulfilling my need for community and connection.

I also work one-on-one with people who feel drawn to explore themselves more deeply—through reflective astrology and conversation.

I’m a lifelong learner, both formally and informally. I hold a Master’s degree in Management from Wilmington University and a Bachelor’s degree in Theatre, with a minor in Astronomy, from the University of Maryland—an unlikely combination that, in many ways, reflects the range of things that continue to interest me.

I’ve often moved through life by following my impulses, even when they didn’t seem logical at the time. Over the years, I’ve come to recognize this as intuition—something I’ve learned to trust while also learning how to navigate.

Even through the most difficult experiences, I’ve found that what brings bitterness can unfold into something meaningful—something even sweet, when given the space to do so.
